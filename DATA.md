# Data Handling & Privacy

This document describes what data the pipeline uses, why, and how it is handled.
The project was built within a UK university research programme (the ESRC
Education Research Programme at UCL) and follows a data-minimisation approach.

## What data is used

| Data | Source | Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|
| Education-sector news/policy articles | Public websites, RSS feeds, Google Alerts (~100 sources) | Public, low-sensitivity |
| Curator section labels | The newsletter curators' editorial decisions | Operational, non-personal |
| Predictions / monitoring logs | Generated by the pipeline | Operational |

The system deliberately uses **public organisational and editorial content only**.
It does **not** collect:

- participant or research-subject data,
- user accounts or user-generated content,
- special-category personal data.

This is a conscious **data-minimisation** choice: public, low-sensitivity source
material keeps the privacy footprint small by design.

## Incidental personal data

Public articles can contain incidental names or quotations (e.g. ministers,
academics, named authors). These are treated as **already-published information in
a professional context**, not as data collected *about* those individuals. No
profiling, enrichment, or linkage of personal data is performed.

## Storage, access & retention

- Operational data and labels are stored in **Supabase (managed Postgres)** under
  access control. Direct operational access is restricted.
- Credentials are managed separately via environment variables and GitHub Actions
  secrets (see [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md)).
- Operational records (predictions, curator decisions, drift/fairness logs) are
  retained for **auditability and reproducibility**, not for user analytics.

## Lawful basis & governance

The work was conducted under the relevant university research-governance, data-
protection and information-security requirements (UK GDPR / Data Protection Act
2018). The precise lawful basis is recorded in the institution's data-protection
documentation and is not restated here.

## What is **not** in this repository

Raw scraped corpora, trained model weights beyond the tracked run artefacts,
operational database dumps, and any personal/assessment material are **gitignored**
and kept out of the public repository (see [`.gitignore`](.gitignore) and
[`PUBLICATION_CHECKLIST.md`](PUBLICATION_CHECKLIST.md)).
