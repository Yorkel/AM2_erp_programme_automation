## Update from PI / Programme

**Anthropology and Education Quarterly - Uncovering Edtech's Embedded Values: Making the Case for Socio-Technical Audits in Ethnographic Inquiry** This open access paper shows how socio-technical audits within ethnographic inquiry can surface the underlying logics of educational technologies. It illuminates how these are interpreted and negotiated in everyday practice.

## Teacher recruitment, retention & development

**DfE - School workforce in England 2025** The annual statistics cover teachers and support staff including age, sex, ethnicity, pupil teacher ratio, retention, pay, subject taught and absence.

**FFT Datalab - Primary school teachers matter for learning, but may have little impact on attendance** Analysis of data from two English multi-academy trusts finds primary teachers have measurable effects on pupils' progress in maths and reading, with effectiveness tending to carry across both subjects. Teacher effects on attendance, by contrast, cluster tightly around zero.

**Schools Week - New heads need more than a one-off qualification, say charities** Teach First and the Reach Foundation have devised a new programme to accelerate aspiring headteachers' careers. Teacher Tapp and SchoolDash research suggests the proportion of deputy and assistant heads aspiring to headship has fallen.

**School Management Plus - Teacher resilience 'depends on system-wide change'** Research by Steph Ainsworth and colleagues examines how schools can mitigate workload pressures through strategies that foster resilience. It concludes that lasting improvement can only come from system-level reform.

## EdTech

**Barnardo's - Call it what it is: The impact of online misogyny on children and young people's attitudes and behaviour** Barnardo's launches a campaign to address online abuse, based on polling 4000 young people across the UK about their experiences.

**The Guardian - The London school that has screen-free days for pupils, teachers and parents** Pupils persuaded teachers to adopt screen-free days at this school. They then got their parents to join in.

**Schools Week - Schools 'expected' to use government route to buy MIS from 2027** Schools will be expected to procure management information systems through a new government framework from September 2027 under a comply or explain approach. The framework, due next summer, aims to reduce costs, protect pupil data and ease integration.

## Political environment and key organisations

**DfE - Attendance mentors intervention: year 2 and 3 evaluation** This reports on the final two years of an intervention providing one-to-one support to persistently or severely absent pupils. The Barnardo's-delivered programme ran across five local authorities.

**DfE - Breakfast club early adopters: interim evaluation** This interim evaluation reports on how free breakfast clubs in the early adopter scheme work in practice. It also examines how families experience them.

## Four Nations

**Department of Education Northern Ireland - Givan announces award of professional learning programme for new and aspiring school principals** Education Minister Paul Givan has announced a three-year contract for a professional learning programme for new and aspiring principals. It will be developed and delivered by a consortium of local Higher Education Institutions in Northern Ireland.

**gov.scot - Phone-free learning in Scottish schools** School learning environments across Scotland will become phone free under legislation to be consulted on within the Scottish Government's first 100 days. Refreshed guidance will be published by end of June to encourage schools to bring in restrictions ahead of the law change.

## Research – Practice – Policy

**Headteacher Update - Ofsted: Is it time for a primary-specific inspection framework?** Peter Cansell and Philip Marples argue that a distinct model for primary school and early years inspection is sorely needed.

## What matters in education?

**Nuffield Foundation - 'I love it, but I hate it': Young people's experiences and expectations of growing up digital** This report presents new evidence about the experiences and expectations of the first generation of young people growing up with digital technology in early twenty-first century Britain.

**LGA - SEND reforms can improve children's outcomes but existing system remains 'vulnerable'** Eight in ten councils believe government SEND reforms would lead to better outcomes for children and young people, according to independent analysis commissioned by the LGA and County Councils Network. However, less than half felt the reforms would lead to a more financially sustainable system.
