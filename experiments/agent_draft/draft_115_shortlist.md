## Update from PI / Programme

**Anthropology and Education Quarterly - Uncovering Edtech's Embedded Values: Making the Case for Socio-Technical Audits in Ethnographic Inquiry** A new open access publication shows how socio-technical audits within ethnographic inquiry can surface the underlying logics of educational technologies. It illuminates how these technologies are interpreted and negotiated within everyday practice.

## Teacher recruitment, retention & development

**Schools Week - Secondary teacher job adverts hit a historic low as market contracts** The latest annual recruitment and retention report from Teacher Tapp and SchoolDash shows secondary teacher vacancies have hit their lowest level in the nine years of available data.

**DfE - School workforce in England 2025** Annual statistics on teachers and support staff cover age, sex, ethnicity, pupil teacher ratio, retention, pay, subject taught and absence.

**FFT Datalab - Primary school teachers matter for learning, but may have little impact on attendance** Analysis of data from two English multi-academy trusts finds primary teachers have measurable effects on pupils' progress in maths and reading. Teacher effects on attendance, however, are tightly clustered around zero.

**Schools Week - New heads need more than a one-off qualification, say charities** Teach First and the Reach Foundation have devised a new programme to accelerate aspiring headteachers' careers. Teacher Tapp and SchoolDash research suggests the proportion of deputy and assistant heads aspiring to headship has fallen.

**NFER - A healthy labour market?** This analysis explores how the staffing picture is changing in primary schools.

## EdTech

**Nuffield Foundation - I love it, but I hate it: Young people's experiences and expectations of growing up digital** This report presents new evidence about the experiences and expectations of the first generation growing up with digital technology in early twenty-first century Britain.

**Barnardo's - Call it what it is: The impact of online misogyny on children and young people's attitudes and behaviour** Barnardo's launch a campaign to address online abuse. It is based on polling 4000 young people across the UK about their experiences.

**University of Cambridge - Limitations of technical fixes in AI policy** A blog from Dr Inga Ulnicane argues that policymakers' assumption that AI is a quick technical fix to complex social problems is unlikely to be realised.

**The Guardian - The London school that has screen-free days for pupils, teachers and parents** This piece reports on a school where pupils persuaded teachers to adopt screen free days. They then got their parents to join in.

**Schools Week - Schools expected to use government route to buy MIS from 2027** Schools will be expected to procure management information systems through a new government framework from September 2027 under a comply or explain approach. The framework aims to reduce costs and protect pupil data.

## Political environment and key organisations

**DfE - Breakfast club early adopters: interim evaluation** This report covers how free breakfast clubs in the early adopter scheme work in practice. It also examines how families experience them.

**DfE - Attendance mentors intervention: year 2 and 3 evaluation** This reports on the final two years of an intervention providing one-to-one support to persistently or severely absent young people. The intervention was delivered by Barnardo's across five local authorities.

**DfE - Parental support for a social media minimum age of 16** This is a preliminary analysis of consultation responses from the Growing up in the online world consultation.

**Schools Week - Managed moves could be consigned to the history books** New government guidance preventing oversubscribed schools from prioritising managed moves in admissions could effectively end the practice. Experts warn this may force struggling schools to take on more pupils at risk of permanent exclusion.

**DfE - Experts at Hand: Everything you need to know** This Education Hub blog explains how the Experts at Hand policy offer will bring specialists into schools, early years and post-16 settings so children can get help earlier.

## Four Nations

**gov.scot - Phone-free learning in Scottish schools** School learning environments across Scotland will become phone free under legislation to be consulted on within the Scottish Government's first 100 days. Refreshed guidance will encourage schools to bring in their own restrictions ahead of the law change.

**Department of Education Northern Ireland - Givan announces award of professional learning programme for new and aspiring school principals** Education Minister Paul Givan has announced a three-year contract for a professional learning programme. It will be developed and delivered by a consortium of local Higher Education Institutions in Northern Ireland.

**Children's Commissioner - The Big Future: Speaking to children in Birmingham** The author discusses conversations with primary-aged children in Birmingham as part of the Big Future survey. Children engaged meaningfully with political and civic topics, discussing local issues with awareness of different perspectives.

## Research – Practice – Policy

**Headteacher Update - Ofsted: Is it time for a primary-specific inspection framework?** Peter Cansell and Philip Marples argue that a distinct model for primary school and early years inspection is sorely needed.

**Headteacher Update - Invitation to inspection: A shared quest for improvement** Peter Cansell and Philip Marples set out a radical new model of inspection. Primary schools would choose their inspection date, feedback would be constructive and judgements narrative.

**Schools Week - Inclusion is for life, not just the 31st December** The Difference argues that schools should use the DfE inclusion strategy deadline to embed whole-school inclusion practices rather than treat it as a compliance exercise. It draws on evidence from their Inclusive Leadership Course.

**School Management Plus - Teacher resilience depends on system-wide change** This reports on research by Steph Ainsworth and colleagues on strategies that foster greater teacher resilience. It finds lasting improvement can only come from system-level reforms.

## What matters in education?

**committees.parliament.uk - Children and young people's mental health: committees launch survey and announce first evidence session** The Education and Health and Social Care committees have launched a survey to capture experiences of young adults who have received mental health services. They have also announced the first evidence session of their mental health inquiry.

**LGA - SEND reforms can improve children's outcomes but existing system remains vulnerable** Eight in 10 councils believe government SEND reforms would lead to better outcomes, according to independent analysis commissioned by the Local Government Association and County Councils Network. Less than half felt the reforms would lead to a more financially sustainable system.

**Fair Education Alliance - Five things the government can do to ensure SEND reforms promote educational equity** Tom Symons identifies five ways the proposals could go further to actively promote educational equity. He argues this would give them the best chance to work successfully in practice.

**Schools Week - Secondary pupil numbers fall for first time in a decade** Secondary school pupil numbers have fallen for the first time in a decade, with 3,659,508 pupils in state schools in January 2026. This reflects a population bulge from the 2000s moving out of the school system.

**Unesco - When multilateralism fractures: Reimagining educational cooperation from the Nigerian context** This article sets out lessons learnt from working in Nigeria. It suggests more plural, meaningful and locally grounded pathways for international cooperation.
