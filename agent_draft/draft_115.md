## Update from PI / Programme

## Teacher recruitment, retention & development

**Schools Week - New heads need more than a one-off qualification, say charities**
Teach First and the Reach Foundation have devised a new programme to help accelerate aspiring headteachers' careers. Teacher Tapp and SchoolDash research suggests the proportion of deputy and assistant heads aspiring to headship has fallen.

**Schools Week - Secondary teacher job adverts hit a historic low as market contracts**
The latest annual recruitment and retention report from Teacher Tapp and SchoolDash shows secondary teacher vacancies have hit the lowest level in the nine-year period for which data is available.

**School Management Plus - Teacher resilience depends on system-wide change**
Research by Steph Ainsworth and colleagues examines how schools can mitigate workload pressures with strategies that foster greater resilience. The work concludes that lasting improvement can only come from system-level reforms.

**DfE - School workforce in England 2025**
Annual statistics on teachers and support staff in England, including age, sex, ethnicity, pupil teacher ratio, retention, pay, subject taught and absence.

**BBC News - Falling pupil numbers should lead to smaller class sizes, says union**
Following the DfE report on teacher numbers, the NEU calls for the government to cut class sizes rather than focus on teacher recruitment.

**FFT Education Datalab - Primary school teachers matter for learning, but may have little impact on attendance**
Analysis of data from two English multi-academy trusts finds primary teachers have measurable effects on pupils' progress in maths and reading, with teachers effective in one subject tending to be effective in the other. Teacher effects on attendance cluster around zero.

## EdTech

**Barnardo's - Call it what it is: the impact of online misogyny on children and young people's attitudes and behaviour**
Barnardo's launches a campaign to address online abuse, based on polling 4,000 young people across the UK about their experiences.

**University of Cambridge - Limitations of technical fixes in AI policy**
A blog from Dr Inga Ulnicane on why policymakers' assumption that AI is a quick technical fix to highly complex and uncertain social problems is unlikely to be realised.

**The Guardian - The London school that has screen-free days for pupils, teachers and parents**
Reports on a school where pupils persuaded teachers to adopt screen-free days, then got their parents to join in.

**Schools Week - Schools expected to use government route to buy MIS from 2027**
Schools will be expected to procure management information systems through a new government framework from September 2027 under a comply or explain approach. The framework, due next summer, will list approved suppliers to reduce costs, protect pupil data, and ease integration.

## Political environment and key organisations

**DfE - Experts at Hand: everything you need to know**
Education Hub blog explaining how the Experts at Hand policy offer will bring specialists into schools, early years and post-16 settings so children can get help earlier.

**DfE - Attendance mentors intervention: year 2 and 3 evaluation**
Reports on the final two years of an intervention providing one-to-one support to young people who were persistently or severely absent, delivered by Barnardo's across five local authorities.

**DfE - Breakfast club early adopters: interim evaluation**
Reports on how free breakfast clubs in the early adopter scheme work in practice and how families experience them.

**Schools Week - Managed moves could be consigned to the history books**
New government guidance preventing oversubscribed schools from prioritising managed moves in admissions could effectively end the practice. Experts warn this may force struggling schools to take on more pupils at risk of permanent exclusion.

## Four Nations

**Department of Education Northern Ireland - Givan announces award of professional learning programme for new and aspiring school principals**
Education Minister Paul Givan has announced a three-year contract for a professional learning programme to be developed and delivered by a consortium of local Higher Education Institutions in Northern Ireland.

**Scottish Government - Phone-free learning in Scottish schools**
School learning environments across Scotland will become phone free under legislation to be consulted on within the government's first 100 days. Refreshed guidance is due by the end of June to encourage schools to bring in their own restrictions ahead of the law change.

## Research – Practice – Policy

**Headteacher Update - Ofsted: is it time for a primary-specific inspection framework?**
Peter Cansell and Philip Marples argue that a distinct model for primary school and early years inspection is sorely needed.

## What matters in education?

**Fair Education Alliance - Five things the government can do to ensure SEND reforms promote educational equity**
Tom Symons identifies five ways the proposals could go further to actively promote educational equity and have the best chance of working in practice.

**Nuffield Foundation - I love it, but I hate it: young people's experiences and expectations of growing up digital**
This report presents new evidence about the experiences and expectations of the first generation of young people growing up with digital technology in early twenty-first century Britain.

**Parliamentary committees - Children and young people's mental health: committees launch survey and announce first evidence session**
The Education and Health and Social Care committees have launched a survey to capture experiences of young adults who have received mental health services. They announced the first evidence session of their inquiry into the causes of growing levels of mental ill health.

**Schools Week - Secondary pupil numbers fall for first time in a decade**
Secondary school pupil numbers have fallen for the first time in a decade, with 3,659,508 pupils in state schools in January 2026, down by almost 12,000, as a population bulge moves out of the system. Overall pupil numbers across all schools have declined to 8,920,227.
